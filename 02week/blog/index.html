You Can Now Buy Cannabis-Infused Coffee Pods

Brewbudz, a small start-up in San Diego, created the world's first weed-infused Keurig-compatible pods, allowing you to add the herb to your coffee, tea, and cocoa. The products are already available in Nevada and will soon hit store shelves in Colorado and California. They're 100 percent compostable, cost $7 a pop, and "offer various dosage options to fit every lifestyle," according to the brand's press release. Each pod, whether coffee, tea, or cocoa, can be purchased in 10-, 25-, and 50-milligram doses of delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), the chemical responsible for most of marijuana's high.

New Starbucks in Brooklyn ignites fear that java joint will hurt local coffee shops

Wake up and smell the competition. That was the reaction of some local residents and small business owners to the new Starbucks opening next spring in Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn. While elected leaders were touting the arrival of the big corporate coffee machine to the corner of Broadway and Marcus Garvey Blvd., some java drinkers were percolating about the new proprietors. "It's kind of a waste," said Maritza Colon, 32, a Parks Department employee. "Another coffee shop? Really? If they're trying to help the community that would be good. There are a lot of new faces around here. It's like they're trying to turn Brooklyn into the new Manhattan." Matt Staples, 26, who runs The West, a Williamsburg coffee shop, said the idea of another Starbucks left a bitter taste in his mouth. "I think it's horrible,” Staples said. “It’s awful competition for local coffee places. They're going to undercut all the costs. And their coffee is burnt and terrible. "If it were a mom and pop place I wouldn't be mad.”
