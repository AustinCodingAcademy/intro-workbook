What's this Book Blog about?

As a mother of two boys, currently one in elementary school, and one in high school, I highly encouraged both boys to read. My husband and I were both avid readers of fiction as children, and wanted to pass down that habit. I read aloud each night to both boys, and still do to my younger one. As my older one transitioned to chapter books to read on his own, I read along with whatever he read. It made for great dinner time discussion.

There are so many great youth adult authors out there now, the genre has grown by leaps and bounds. Unfortunately, young adult means different things to different people. I was glad I had the time and inclination to read along with my older son. Sometimes I read ahead of him when he wanted to read something I'd not read before. I was able to say, "Hmm, not just yet." I started this blog for my own delight of books, and also in hopes that some parents find it useful to gauge whether their child is ready for THOSE books.

Please see my rating system below. It's pretty simple. If you have any questions about any particular book listed here, of course you can email me, or talk to your local librarian.


Rating System

  • L - Language
  • S - Sexual Situations
  • V - Violence
  • Y - Youth Safe

The Sheriff of Yrnameer by Michael Rubens

****

In this rollicking space adventure, our not-quite anti-hero has to decide between saving his own skin and doing good... or does he? Down on his luck Cole finds himself in the middle of a rescue mission to the most idyllic of all places... the rumored last of the Your Name Here colonies. But he's got Kenneth after him for gambling debts, and Kenneth isn't the easiest creature in the universe to shake off. Can Yrnameer be the place that saves him? Can Cole be the human that Yrnameer needs to save it?

Youth Rating: VLS, Older Teens Only!

Avian by Nicole Conway

*****

Excellent second book of the dragonrider series by Ms. Conway. Continues the journey of the half-breed gray elf and human, and his friends. His patron has suffered an attack and can no longer ride. His new trainer teaches him even better. The ranks of the dragonriders become divided over additional threats. Are his new powers to blame or is there some other source of the wild animals attacking everyone? Very suspenseful, and the ending is a cliff-hanger. Can't wait to read the final book!

Youth Rating: Y

Fledging by Nicole Conway

*****

Fantastic first of three books about dragons and the young people who ride them. This first book follows a half-breed, child of a grey elf woman and human father, who is left behind at 12 when his elvish mother dies. He goes to live, unwanted, at his human father's house. Small and frail for his age, he is misunderstood and bullied. When his craftsmen father finally takes him to the dragon's training ground, he is drawn to them, and unbelievably to the established hierachy, chosen by a wild, untamed dragon. His life changes dramatically as a dragonrider, and he learns to soar!

Youth Rating: Y
