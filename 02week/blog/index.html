1. Cisco: How Cisco Uses Personally Identifiable Information

Cisco is an American multinational technology company that designs, manufactures, and sells networking equipment. Just like most large companies, Cisco also has a way of using personally identifiable information(PII) for its customers. PPI presents a variety of consumer protection issues that have either arisen or become more urgent because of the Internet. Trust among online consumers is heavily dependent on the consumer's behavior, as it has been estimated as much as "87% of consumers refused to give information to websites because they were too personal or unnecessary" (Roberts, Zahay, 2013).

Examples of Risk for Consumer Online Security Concerns for Businesses:

  • Identity Thieves -use the information they find online to gain access to customer's bank accounts, have the possibility to destroy credit ratings.
  • Phishing -occurs when a website becomes hacked or is illegitimate. Such websites as eBay and PayPal are two of the most often targeted websites related to phishing scams.
  • Social Media Websites -Many popular Social Media Websites such as Facebook are also targets for scams.

3. SEO: What is Search Engine Optimization and Why is it Important for Businesses?

Before jumping into analysis of this marketing techniques, it is important to know the concepts behind search engines, search optimization, keywords, and social media marketing aspects. Search engines provide consumers with a way to search for what they are trying to find within the internet. The entire process of getting listed on search engines so consumers can find you is called search engine optimization (SEM). The concept behind search engine optimization for businesses is to form an internet marketing that seeks to promote websites by increasing their visibility in search engine pages. There are two basic forms of SEM:

Two Forms of SEM:

  • Search Engine Optimization: - This process refers to the design of a website and its content through which search engines find the website without being paid to do so.
  • Pay-Per-Click -is an internet advertising model used to direct traffic to websites, in which advertisers pay the publisher, typically a website owner or a host of website, when the ad is clicked. It is defined simply as the amount spent to get an advertisement clicked.
