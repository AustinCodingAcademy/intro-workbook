All About Shelby

Personal

Hello! My name is Shelby Inloes. I hope you take time to get to know me. This page only scratches the surface of all that makes up a persons life and personality, but its worth a shot!

I was born and raised in a small town in Ohio and somehow found myself 24 years later in beautiful Austin, Texas. Somewhere inbetween I also spent some time in Hattiesburg, Mississippi for schooling. All of that traveling around has led me to love discovering new places, but love going home even more.

Living in Austin gives me plenty of oppoturnity to enjoy my previous hobbies as well as find new ones. Experiencing new things is the greatest reason I love to move to a new city. Austin has it all - a great food scene, water sports, hiking/biking trails, and culture. If I am not at the park with my crazy dog Zoey, I am playing sand volleyball or finding some nice eats (sometimes all in one night). The other best part about Austin is the ammount of opportunity there is here - for anything! I love that I have started to experience new types of people that have shown me oppoturnities for new passions in life.

Professional

Starting off with the typical I went to school 'here' and studied 'this' I would rather start by saying the where and why. When I was a young innocent high schooler I thought that college would be a chance for me to explore life on my own and just magically have a good job right when I graduated. I endured 18 years of cold winters and I was having no more of that, so I picked up and moved 11 hours to south Mississppi. Suprizingly, not as big of a culture shock as you would think with me coming from the midwest (my high school was literally surrounded by cornfields and I lived next to a cow pasture). I also decided I would cheer, join a sorority, and study nursing. Don't remember seeing nursing anywhere? Yeah that didn't last long. I ended up graduating with a degree in Kinesiology because I loved learning about how our bodies worked and well - I was a juinior when I switched majors so I had to choose something similar...

Anyways, after graduation I picked up and moved again. When I got to Austin I had several jobs before landing one actually using my degree. I got to work with people, analytically think, and wear comfortable clothes to work! It was great, but still left more to be desired. I wanted to make something... tangible. When I begin to think of what I wanted long term I found out that I want a career where I can build and say 'I did this'. So much gratification and job satisfaction comes from knowing you are important. There I found myself introduced to the world of coding and heres were I begin my new journey.

My passions revolve around a lifetime of learning. Although I went to school for something other than computer science, I am not diving into the world of web development. The funniest part to that transistion is realizing just how many similarities you find within different fields. I had a passion for the human body (hence - the kinesiology degree and phsychology minor) and how it worked. I was able to see and feel what they were teaching in class all around me. Now that I have started learning more about the tech world, it all seems so simliar. We are all just here trying to learn more about a subject - how it works, how we can manipulate it, and the incredible things we can do with it.
