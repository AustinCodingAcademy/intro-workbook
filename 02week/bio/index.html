Speaking of Circuit of the Americas, the last hobby I'd like to talk about, which is actually more of a passion, is road racing. If you're unfamiliar with road racing, basically there are events that take place at race tracks (circuit-type tracks, not drag strips) that which allow for people like you and me to sign up with an appropriate vehicle and you actually drive on the track with other drivers in their cars. Usually you begin with an instructor riding along with the driver to make sure the driver is aware of the flags and know how to react to each flag, making sure they are aware of their surroundings, and that the driver has a basic understanding of how the car will react when the driver starts to push their limits. It's a great place to truly learn your car and how it will react in certain situations, moreover learning how to react accordingly. Eventually you can work your way up to driving more competively where there is open-passing (in the beginning stages of road racing, you have to signal to other drivers before they are allowed to pass you), and you're either competing for time against other drivers/cars that are in your given class, or even get into wheel-to-wheel racing where you're racing for position. I have a bucket-list of race tracks around North America that I would love to drive my Honda S2000 at.
