About Me

My name is Kiera Wolfe. In the Fall of 2014, I began taking ASL classes at Austin Community College (ACC), an hour long commute. I was still in high school, and appreciated the opportunity to get a head start and begin my incipient college education.

Since I began the interpreting program, I've never learned so much about a single culture and language. With each class, observation, interaction, my cognizance grows and I fall more in love with ASL. I am blessed to be allowed into the Deaf community with open arms. I take every opportunity to volunteer and give back.

In the Fall of 2016, I completed ASL 6, the final ASL class in the ACC interpreting program. At this time, I was working as a part time department manger at Hobby Lobby, as well as taking additional classes and volunteering weekly at Texas School for the Deaf. My anticipated graduation date from the interpreting program is May of 2018.
