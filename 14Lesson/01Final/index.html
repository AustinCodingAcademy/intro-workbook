Therapy

The most important thing to look for when choosing a therapist is how comfortable you feel with that person.

Therapy is about forming a team with your choice of professional to help you meet your goals. Whether you're working on feeling better, improving your relationships, or healing from past hurts, you will accomplish more in therapy if you feel like your therapist "gets" you.

In addition, therapy will be more helpful to you if you view our time together as a space to practice new, more effective ways of communicating and behaving. Relating to ourselves and to others is often messy and complicated and sometimes we all need a compassionate person to coach us to become the best version of ourselves.

I draw from many different therapeutic theories, including cognitive, behavioral, solution-focused, and trauma focused strategies. If you have found a particular strategy to work particularly well for you, please let me know.

About Tricia

My approach to change involves listening to all the parts of you, starting with the part that wants something different and better, so that we can work together to address what gets in the way. I'm a firm believer in the power of the therapeutic relationship to transform my clients' experiences of themselves, their relationships, and their lives.

I work with adolescents and adults in individual, couples, and group sessions. My experience includes psychotherapy with families and relationships in crisis, outpatient behavioral health treatment, and social services program development. I have a BA from the Evergreen State College in Olympia, WA, and an MSSW from the University of Texas at Austin.

I believe that my growth and education as a person and as a therapist is vital to my ability to be helpful to my clients. My method is grounded in ongoing study of evidence-based theories for working with the entire family system, improving the stability and flexibility of romantic relationships, dealing with difficult and unwanted behaviors, and improving mood symptoms.
